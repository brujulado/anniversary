I'LL SAY I MET YOU IN A MUSEUM

I'll say I met you in a museum.
You appeared between dark rooms and colorful photographs,
while I was listening to sorrowful songs.

I’ll say that you looked secretly at me in the first room,
while I was looking at an empty canvas,
that was depicting a loveless life.

I’ll say that you hold my hand on the second floor,
after I gave you a nervous smile,
in front of the sculpture of a galaxy.

I’ll say that you kissed me in the third section,
where the lights exhibition had place,
and everything was flashing like my eyes.

I’ll say that we said goodbye in the souvenirs area,
after we told each other a thousand stories,
and planned a life together.

But I was taught not to tell lies.
This doesn’t feel right.
I think I’ll go with the truth.

I’ll say that I met you on the internet.
We went for a bike ride on a summer afternoon.
Next to a gorgeous lake and a haunted forest.

I’ll say that you invited me to your place.
We had beers on the coziest terrace,
and that my nose started to bleed out of nowhere.

I’ll say that I fell in love the first time you hugged me into your arms.
That we made love wildly and passionately.
Or maybe we should keep those memories to ourselves.

I’ll say that you were such a gentleman.
You said you want to meet me again.
I remember that first day like yesterday.

Now I could say so many things after two years together:

I’ll say that I listen to songs thinking about you.
I’ll say that I talk a lot about you with myself.
I’ll say that I write you poems and love letters.
I’ll say that I’ll go anywhere with you.
I’ll say that I love you so much.
